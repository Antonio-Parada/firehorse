About Bryan Rouse

Bryan Rouse L.Ac., MSOM, Dipl.Ac. (NCCAOM) has been offering his experience and expertise in Acupuncture and Eastern Medicine for over 25 years. Bryan completed his Masters of Science Degree in Eastern Medicine at the prestigious graduate school, Southwest Acupuncture College in Boulder, Colorado in the year 2000. Prior to receiving High Honors in Colorado, Bryan graduated with a Bachelor's Degree in Arts and Sciences at the University of Vermont in 1988.

Continuing this earlier path of studies, Bryan worked as an Emergency Medical Technician/Paramedic in his hometown area of Southern Vermont, with his focused experience at the Mount Snow Ski Resort- Mountain Sports Medicine Clinic.

After graduating from Southwest Acupuncture College, Bryan owned and practiced in Denver, Colorado for over 15 years. This busy practice, worked extensively in Sports Medicine and women's health and infertility. Noticeably working with the Denver Broncos Football Club and the city of Denver community. In addition to his extensive experience in Sports Medicine and trauma, Bryan is passionate in treating Women's health- hormone imbalances, infertility, depression/anxiety. Often he treats and manages acute and chronic pain, insomnia, back pain, headaches/migraine, tinnitus, digestive disorders, low energy, and ongoing care for cancer and aging.

Being a native Vermonter, it was very inevitable for Bryan to move back to his roots in Vermont to practice his healthcare craft of Holistic Medicine. Bryan currently practices in Vermont holding offices at the Bennington Center for Healing Arts in Bennington, Vermont and most recently, in Stowe, Vermont, above Little River Chiropractic & Wellness Center.

Bryan's emphasis is on treating the body as a whole- mind/body, and also focuses on the specific complaints of his patients using several modalities that include: Acupuncture, herbs and supplements, diet & nutrition, traditional glass cupping, moxibustion, electrical stimulation/TENS, and Guasha

What is Acupuncture? ☯️

Acupuncture has its roots and history in the eastern countries of Asia. This scope of health involves stimulating specific points on the body often with very thin sterile needles to promote healing and balance.

Acupuncture is used for various conditions, including chronic and acute pain, nausea, fatigue, and several other mental and physical disharmonies. Acupuncture mainly works by stimulating the body's natural healing response. When pressure and fine needles are inserted into specific acupoints, its benefit is to release chemicals in the brain, spinal cord, and muscles that can help relieve pain and promote well being.

If the fear of needles for treatment is present to the patient, the practitioner may use other modalities such as Acupressure, moxibustion (heat therapy), cupping & Guasha (Massage techniques) to achieve healthy benefits.
